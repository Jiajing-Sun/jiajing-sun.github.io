On **October 27, 2025**, I had the honor of organizing a talk by **Professor Wu Jie** from the **Beijing Yanqihu Institute of Applied Mathematics (BIMSA)**, as part of the **Department of Statistics and Data Science's Special Invited Talk Series (Session 7)**. The talk, titled **"Topology Meets Statistics in Data"**, was held at the **University of Chinese Academy of Sciences**, Zhongguancun Campus.

![Wu Jie Seminar](https://jiajing-sun.github.io/images/WuJie-seminar.png)

### **Abstract**:
In this talk, **Professor Wu Jie** provided an introductory lecture on cutting-edge **topological approaches to data science**. The presentation offered an overview of **topological data analysis** and **GLMY theory**, showcasing successful applications by integrating **GLMY theory** with modern **statistics**, driven by researchers at BIMSA.

### **Speaker Bio**:
**Wu Jie** is a renowned **researcher** at the **Beijing Yanqihu Institute of Applied Mathematics (BIMSA)**. He received his **Ph.D.** in Mathematics from the **University of Rochester** and conducted **postdoctoral research** at the **Mathematical Sciences Research Institute, University of California, Berkeley**. He previously served as a **tenured professor** in the **Department of Mathematics at the National University of Singapore**. His research focuses on **algebraic topology** and its applications. 

Professor Wu Jie has made significant contributions in **algebraic topology**, notably establishing fundamental links between **homotopy groups** and **braid group theory**. His work has also explored the applications of **topological theories** in fields such as **complex networks**, **drug design**, and **material science**. His team has successfully applied **topological data analysis** to various domains, such as **microbial interactions** and **drug research**.
